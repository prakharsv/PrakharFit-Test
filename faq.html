About Prakhar

I am an ACE Certified Personal Trainer and Sports Nutrition Specialist based in Indore, India. I hold a Level 4 CPT, an EREPS Level 4 membership, a SPEFL-SC certification, and additional qualifications in Olympic lifting, kettlebell training, plyometrics, suspension training, and CPR.

But credentials alone are not enough. I spent years as a practicing advocate before becoming a coach. At my heaviest I weighed 120 kg, and I lost close to 30 kg using the same evidence-based principles I now apply to my clients. I understand what it takes from the inside, not just from a textbook.

Coaching is my primary profession. I left legal practice specifically to do this full-time. When you work with me, you are not fitting into someone's evenings and weekends. You have access to a coach who is fully committed to this work.

I am based in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, but both of my programs are delivered entirely online. I coach clients across India. All communication happens through WhatsApp and email, and workout and nutrition plans are delivered digitally. You do not need to be in Indore, or even in the same time zone, for this to work.

Online Coaching Process

After you fill out the contact form and we agree on which program is right for you, the process looks like this:

Week 1: You complete a detailed intake form covering your health history, food preferences, schedule, training experience, and goals. I use this to build your initial workout and nutrition plan.

Ongoing: Your workout plan is delivered digitally with full exercise descriptions, sets, reps, and coaching notes. On the Complete Transformation, I check in weekly via WhatsApp to review progress and adjust. On the Essentials Plan, I am reachable by email and update your plan monthly.

The initial discussion typically happens via WhatsApp voice note or a short call, depending on your preference. Ongoing coaching is primarily WhatsApp and email-based.

Form review on the Complete Transformation is done via video clip. You send me a clip of your exercise, I review it and send specific feedback. This has worked reliably for every client I have coached online.

On the Complete Transformation, WhatsApp access is available Monday to Saturday from 9am to 9pm. I aim to respond within a few hours. For non-urgent questions, I always respond by end of day. On the Essentials Plan, email responses are within 48 hours.

Life happens. Missing a session or a week does not end your progress. On the Complete Transformation, I will know via the weekly check-in, and we will adjust accordingly. There is no judgment. The goal is never perfection. It is consistency over time.

Nutrition Questions

No. This is one of the questions I get most often and the answer is always the same. I am from Indore. I understand Indian food, Indian household cooking, and the cultural role food plays in daily life. Your plan will be built around real Indian foods across vegetarian, vegan, or non-vegetarian preferences.

What changes is the structure, the portions, and the balance of macronutrients, not the food itself.

Tracking is the most reliable tool for hitting your targets, especially at the beginning. The level of tracking I ask for depends on your goal and your appetite for detail. For fat loss or muscle gain with specific targets, tracking macros is important in the early weeks. Over time, many clients move to a less rigorous approach while maintaining results.

I keep supplement recommendations minimal and evidence-based. The ones with the most consistent research support are creatine monohydrate, protein powder as a convenience tool, and vitamin D if you are deficient, which most Indians are.

I do not recommend fat burners, testosterone boosters, or anything with weak or no scientific backing. Supplements fill gaps in an already solid diet. They do not replace one.

Yes. Paneer, dahi, eggs, lentils, legumes, soya chunks, and dairy-based options can all be used effectively. I have built vegetarian plans that hit 150g of protein per day without relying on supplements for the majority of intake.

For vegan clients, the options are slightly narrower but still workable with the right combination of soya, legumes, lentils, tofu, and plant-based protein supplements where needed.

A plan that does not account for real life is not a good plan. I will give you practical strategies for eating out, attending functions, and managing social eating without derailing your progress. The goal is to make this work in the context of your actual life.
